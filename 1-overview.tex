\paragraph{Abstract and project goal:}
Image Difference Captioning (IDC) requires generating a natural-language description that highlights subtle differences between a visually similar image pair. The ADPO framework trains an IDC captioning policy via pairwise preference optimisation with Direct Preference Optimisation (DPO) under a Bradley–Terry–Luce model, strengthened by adversarially retrieved hard negative captions. While DPO simplifies optimisation by avoiding explicit reward modelling, IDC performance remains sensitive to the quality of negative samples, and prior work shows vulnerabilities in preference-based objectives under adversarial settings. 

In this project, we replace DPO-based policy updates with on-policy reinforcement learning, comparing Proximal Policy Optimisation (PPO) and Group Relative Policy Optimisation (GRPO), where GRPO removes the critic via group-based baselines for improved efficiency. We further integrate a contrastive objective to align visually faithful captions in embedding space while separating misaligned or counterfactual ones. The resulting policies will be evaluated on standard IDC benchmarks (BLEU, METEOR, ROUGE-L, CIDEr) against DPO/ADPO baselines.

\paragraph{TL;DR (``Too Long; Didn't Read"):}
We replace DPO-based IDC training with on-policy RL (PPO/GRPO) and add contrastive objectives to improve robustness and visual grounding.

\subsection{Motivation}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Why is the problem interesting?} IDC is a challenging vision-language task requiring fine-grained visual grounding to distinguish small differences between highly similar image pairs. It naturally fits a preference-based learning framework, making it suitable for comparing DPO and reinforcement learning approaches.

    \item \textbf{Critical challenges.} 
    Challenge 1: Performance depends heavily on the quality of negative captions; weak negatives reduce learning effectiveness. 
    Challenge 2: Stable RL for sequence generation is difficult—PPO improves stability but introduces additional computational overhead through actor–critic training. 
    Challenge 3: Preference-based optimisation is vulnerable to reward misspecification and adversarial data, leading to potential alignment issues.

    \item \textbf{Justify why the problem remains open or unsolved.} Existing IDC methods rely on supervised fine-tuning or DPO-based optimisation, both of which are sensitive to dataset biases or negative sampling quality. Although ADPO improves performance using adversarial negatives, it still inherits limitations of DPO. This motivates exploring whether on-policy RL methods like PPO and GRPO can better utilise preference signals while improving robustness. Additionally, contrastive language–image alignment offers a principled way to enforce visual consistency.

    \item \textbf{State-of-the-art methods.} ADPO is the strongest IDC baseline due to adversarial hard-negative retrieval combined with DPO. PPO is the standard RL baseline, while GRPO is a recent efficient alternative for large-scale preference optimisation.
\end{itemize}
